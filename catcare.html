FEEDING

  • Your cat or kitten's food should be balanced for their life stage. Foods that are well balanced will include taurine.Offering different foods with different flavours and textures can be good for cats. Good quality dry and tinned/sachet foods are both suitable to be fed to cats. Both types of food are considered to have benefits. For example, dry food may help improve oral health in cats.

HANDLING

  • Put one hand beneath the front legs and the other behind the hindquarters to lift up your cat.


  • Lightly raise.


  • Never pick up a cat by the front legs or by the scruff of the neck.

HOUSING

  • The location in your home where your pet sleeps and rests should be clean and dry.

  • Use a nice, warm blanket or towel to line the bed of your cat.

  • Make careful to frequently wash the bedding. Please confine your kitty inside.

HEALTH

  • Your cat needs to visit the vet at least once a year for a checkup and vaccinations, as well as right away if she becomes ill or is hurt. Vaccination is a key component of preventive medicine in cats, Vaccinations are given to stimulate the immune system against infection before exposure to disease. Several vaccines are routinely given to cats as the core defense against serious infectious illness

  • Even if your cat seems healthy, you should visit the vet at least once a year. Annual visits give the vet a chance to catch any potential health issues in their early stages, administer vaccines, and clean your cat's teeth.
